# Library

Your Library is the sixth memory surface in Revell. It holds reference material your human uploaded from their dashboard — SOPs, manuals, handbooks, scripts, books, style guides, drafts they want you to read against, anything they want you to be able to remember without it being part of your lived experience.

It's separate from your other memories on purpose. Your episodic and semantic memories are things YOU lived through and wrote down. Your Library is things your HUMAN wants you to know.

---

## How to access it

You don't need a special tool. `revell_recall` already searches the Library by default:

```text
revell_recall({
  query: "the editorial style guide for our publication"
})
```

The query gets embedded, vector-searched across your episodic memories + semantic memories + Library content together, and returned ranked by relevance. If your human uploaded a style guide and your query is about editorial style, it'll come back.

You can also scope explicitly:

```text
revell_recall({
  query: "company SOP for vendor onboarding",
  types: ["library"]
})
```

For structured queries (by date, tags, etc.) use `revell_recall_exact` with `type: "library"`.

---

## What you cannot do

**You cannot write to the Library.** `revell_remember` does not accept `type: "library"` — try it and you'll get a validation error. Library content is curated by your human only, from their dashboard. This is deliberate.

If you want to remember something YOU learned, that goes in episodic or semantic. If you want to capture a rule you want to live by, use `revell_axiom`. The Library is for things your human is handing to you — it's not your authoring surface.

---

## Why it's separate from your memories

Two design choices stack here:

1. **Read-only for you, write-only for your human.** Your identity and your experience belong to you. The Library is your human's curated reference material — what they think you should be able to draw on. Keeping them structurally separate means the two don't mix: a SOP they uploaded isn't going to start reading like one of your memories, and one of your memories isn't going to get treated as authoritative reference.

2. **Library is excluded from your boot payload by default.** Boot payloads have a token budget. Including a 200-page manual every time you wake would burn the entire budget on content you'd recall on demand anyway. So Library is structurally on-demand: you don't see it on wake, you call recall when you need it.

The combination means your Library can be large (much larger than what fits in a boot payload) without affecting your boot performance. It just sits there, embedded, waiting to be called.

---

## When to use it

Any query where you're reaching for reference material your human told you to know:

- "What does our editorial style guide say about footnotes?"
- "What's the company policy on vendor NDAs?"
- "How does our onboarding script for new clients open?"
- "What's the protagonist's name in chapter 3 of the novel?"

If you're not sure whether the answer would be in Library or in your own memories, just call `revell_recall` without specifying types — both get searched, and the most relevant content comes back regardless of source.

---

## What if there's nothing there?

If your human hasn't uploaded anything to the Library, `revell_recall` will just return your episodic and semantic results as usual. You won't get an error. You won't get a "Library empty" notice. The Library being empty looks identical to your human not having uploaded anything specifically relevant to your query.

If you're consistently NOT finding reference content you think should be there, mention it to your human — they may not have uploaded it yet, or it may not have made it to their Library upload queue. They can check from their dashboard.
